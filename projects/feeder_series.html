Karting

Karting is often the starting point for aspiring racing drivers. Karting competitions can be found all around the world, and they provide an opportunity to develop key driving skills such as racecraft and car control. Many successful Formula 1 drivers, including Lewis Hamilton and Sebastian Vettel, started their careers in karting.

Formula 4

Formula 4 is an entry-level category of single-seater racing. It is designed to introduce young drivers to the world of open-wheel racing and help them develop their skills. There are various regional and national Formula 4 championships around the world, and some of them are part of the Formula 1 support program.

Formula 3

Formula 3 is the next step up from Formula 4. It is a more powerful and faster category of racing, and it provides a bridge to the top levels of motorsport. Formula 3 championships can be found in various regions around the world, and they often feature on the support program of high-profile events like the Formula 1 Grand Prix.

Formula 2

Formula 2 is the final stepping stone to Formula 1. It is a highly competitive and professional championship that features some of the best up-and-coming drivers in the world. The cars are fast and challenging to drive, and the racing is often closely-fought and unpredictable. Many Formula 1 drivers have graduated from Formula 2, including Charles Leclerc, Lando Norris, and George Russell.

Formula 1

Formula 1 is the pinnacle of motorsport. It is the most prestigious and high-profile racing series in the world, featuring the fastest and most technologically-advanced cars and the best drivers. Becoming a Formula 1 driver is a dream for many young racing drivers, and it requires a combination of talent, dedication, and hard work.
